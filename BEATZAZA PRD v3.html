01Summary

What we're building

BEATZAZA.COM is a single-producer auction house for exclusive Afrobeats instrumentals. Only OBV (the site owner) lists beats. Every beat is a true one-of-one: when its auction ends and payment settles, the buyer receives the master, the stems, and the signed exclusive license — and the listing is permanently removed from the public site.

The defining mechanic is a 30-minute countdown that begins when the first bid lands, paired with a 2-minute anti-snipe extension and a public live leaderboard. This compresses time, creates social proof, and converts passive listeners into active bidders.

Audio and deliverables are hosted on Google Drive — preview MP3s are public on a "anyone with the link" Drive folder; masters, stems and licenses live in a restricted Drive folder and are shared with the winner's email for a 7-day download window, then revoked. Payment is crypto-only via Plisio (BTC, ETH, USDT, LTC, etc.). If the winning bidder doesn't pay within 24 hours, the auction silently re-offers to the next bidder at their original bid amount.

Spotify-style streaming made beats infinitely available and infinitely cheap. BEATZAZA.COM makes them scarce again — and turns each sale into a public event where bids are public but payment outcomes are not.

02Problem & Opportunity

The producer economy is a race to the bottom

  1. Race to the bottom on price. BeatStars-style marketplaces commoditize beats; the same instrumental gets sold to dozens of artists for $30 a pop with weak licensing.
  2. No urgency, no event. A beat sits in a catalog forever. There's no moment that pulls fans, A&Rs, or rival producers in to compete.
  3. Exclusive sales are friction-heavy. A serious "exclusive" deal usually means DMs, invoices, and Dropbox links — slow, untrackable, and easy to get scammed on.
  4. No enforceable credit. Even when artists buy "exclusive" beats, producers rarely get the credit they're owed across platforms.
  5. Card-processor friction for crypto-native buyers. Many high-spend artists and A&Rs internationally prefer to pay in crypto — card-only auctions filter them out.

BEATZAZA.COM replaces all of that with a single producer-controlled storefront where every beat is a timed, one-shot event with public stakes, a contractually-enforced producer credit, and crypto-only settlement.

03Goals & Non-Goals

What success looks like

Goals (in scope for v1)

  • Allow only OBV to list beats for auction.
  • Allow any visitor to preview, place bids, and (if winning) settle in crypto via Plisio.
  • Make every beat a true one-of-one: removed from the site at auction close.
  • Deliver master WAV + stems ZIP + signed license PDF through Google Drive share links granted to the winner's email for 7 days.
  • Enforce a public producer credit clause ("Produced by OBV") in every license.
  • Implement a private payment cascade: if the top bidder doesn't pay within 24 h, silently re-offer to the runner-up at their original bid. Repeat down the chain until someone pays.
  • Create urgency and social pressure via a 30-min countdown + live leaderboard.
  • Provide OBV a private Studio for catalog, live auctions, cascade management, and a full Settings panel for site-wide configuration.
  • Serve public Terms / Privacy / FAQ pages whose copy authorizes the cascade mechanic without disclosing the pricing of re-offers.

Non-goals (explicitly out for v1)

  • Multi-producer / open marketplace.
  • Fiat / card payments.
  • Subscription, leasing, or non-exclusive licensing tiers.
  • Native mobile apps. (Mobile web only.)
  • Built-in DAW or stem editor.
  • Public bidder profiles, follows, or social graph.

06Core Flows

The auction lifecycle

OBV lists a beat
Authenticated owner pastes four URLs from Google Drive: preview MP3 (public folder, "Anyone with the link · Viewer"), master WAV, stems ZIP, license PDF template (all three in the restricted folder). Adds title, BPM, key, genre, duration, starting bid. Listing goes live immediately with status OPEN.
Visitors browse & preview
Any visitor streams the watermarked preview directly from the public Drive folder via the site's audio player. No account needed.
First bid starts the clock
Bidder signs in (Google / Apple / magic-link) and creates a unique @handle. No card pre-auth. The bid is a binding commitment to pay if they win. Auction transitions OPEN → LIVE and the 30-min countdown begins.
Competition heats up
Each new bid must clear the current top bid by at least the minimum increment ($5 default). Leaderboard re-ranks live; previous top bidder receives an instant outbid email + on-site toast.
Anti-snipe extension
If a valid bid is placed in the final 2 minutes, the timer resets to 2:00. Repeats until 2 full minutes pass without a new bid.
Auction closes
Timer hits zero. Listing removed from the public site within 1 second. Server computes the cascade chain: each unique bidder's best bid, sorted high → low. The #1 slot becomes the current claimant.
Invoice sent to the current claimant
Server calls Plisio's createInvoice with the claimant's bid amount and emails them a payment link. Invoice supports BTC / ETH / USDT / LTC / BCH / DOGE / XMR / BNB / SOL / TRX.
Crypto payment
Claimant pays from their wallet. Plisio waits for the configured confirmations (default 2) then sends a webhook with status completed.
Deliverables released
On webhook receipt, server uses Drive API to share master.wav, stems.zip, and the stamped license.pdf with the buyer's email for 7 days, then emails them three download links.
CASCADE · if claimant doesn't pay in 24 h
Server cron marks the current claimant as unpaid, advances the cascade index to the next bidder, creates a new Plisio invoice at that bidder's original bid amount, and sends them a normal "You won — please pay" email. The runner-up has no visibility that they were not the first to be offered the beat.
End state
Either someone in the chain pays (sale recorded; deliverables released) or every bidder defaults (rare; OBV manually re-lists). Public records show the original top bid as the "sold for" price regardless of where in the chain the actual payment came from.

09Payments & Cascade

Crypto-only, with silent fallback

Plisio integration

All payments settle in crypto via Plisio. After auction close, server calls Plisio's createInvoice endpoint:

POST https://plisio.net/api/v1/invoices/new api_key: <PLISIO_SECRET_API_KEY> source_currency: USD source_amount: 250.00 currency: BTC (or buyer's chosen coin) order_name: BZZ-XXXXXXXX order_number: <deliveryId> callback_url: https://beatzaza.com/api/plisio/webhook email: <winner_email> ↓ Returns: {invoice_url, wallet_hash, amount, qr_code_url}

The buyer is emailed invoice_url and given paymentWindowHours (default 24) to settle. Plisio sends a webhook on each status change (new → pending → completed). On completed we trigger deliverable release.

The cascade — admin-only design

When an auction closes, server builds the cascade chain from the bid history:

  1. Group bids by @handle; keep only each bidder's highest bid.
  2. Sort descending by amount.
  3. Map to cascade entries: {handle, email, bid, attempt, at}.
  4. Set currentClaimantIndex = 0, status pending.

Example cascade (admin-only view in Studio):

#HandleTheir best bidStatus
1@santino.mx$285✗ no-pay
2@kofi_beats$265◴ pending
3@drumwolf$245queued
4@lola.studio$175queued
5@vibez_4_days$160queued

In the scenario above, @santino.mx (the public-facing "winner" at $285) defaulted. The system silently re-offered the beat to @kofi_beats at $265 — their original bid amount. If @kofi_beats pays, the sale's actual collected revenue is $265 even though the public Sold list still shows $285.

Disclosure

The cascade mechanic is never exposed to bidders. The new claimant's email is worded as a standard "You won" notification. They have no in-product or in-email signal that they were not the original highest bidder. This design choice is documented in Public Terms §4 in legally-defensible language without disclosing the pricing mechanic.

Settlement

  • Plisio's USD-equivalent quote is locked at invoice creation time and held for ~30 minutes; if the buyer doesn't broadcast a transaction by then, they'll need to refresh the invoice.
  • Confirmations are configurable (1–6). Default is 2 for fastest UX with reasonable safety.
  • On successful payment, server stores the on-chain transaction hash in the sale record for audit purposes.

10Delivery & Email

Closing the loop

Within 60 seconds of Plisio webhook reporting completed, the buyer receives a transactional email containing:

  • Beat title, BPM, key, genre, final price (the buyer's bid amount).
  • Three stacked download buttons, each pointing at a Drive-shared file:
    • {title}.wav — 24-bit / 48 kHz master.
    • {title}-stems.zip — drums, bass, melody, perc, 808, etc.
    • {title}-license.pdf — exclusive license with the buyer's name + delivery ID auto-stamped.
  • Delivery ID (format BZZ-{base36 timestamp}) for support & chargeback evidence.
  • Friendly "house rules" block summarizing ownership, credit clause, download window, and breach consequence.

Drive access is granted via the Drive API (files.permissions.create with role reader, type user, email = buyer's email, expirationTime = +7 days). After 7 days, a cron job calls files.permissions.delete to revoke access.

Same delivery is recorded in Studio → Sales log. The "View" action re-renders the email and re-grants Drive access on demand in case of bounces or accidental revocation within the window.

Email provider configurable in Settings (Resend / Postmark / SES / SendGrid). Dedicated sending subdomain (deliveries.beatzaza.com) with SPF + DKIM + DMARC.

11License Terms

The license clauses below are shown to bidders before they bid (in the bid modal & sidebar), after they win (in the email), and embedded into the signed PDF.

  2. Required credit. Every commercial release of any derivative work (full song, remix, sample, sync placement) must credit "Produced by OBV" verbatim in: (a) the song title or its parenthetical, AND (b) the platform's "Credits" or "Description" section, on every platform where the work is published.
  3. Download window. Drive share links to the master, stems, and license PDF are valid for 7 days from the moment of payment confirmation. After that, sharing is revoked and the buyer must contact OBV for re-issue.

  5. One-of-one. The buyer is the sole licensee. OBV will not license the same instrumental to any other party.

License text is editable in Settings → License. The credit phrase ("Produced by OBV") is also editable.

12Storage Architecture

Two Drive folders, one Google account

All audio and PDF assets live in Google Drive, in two folders under a dedicated Google account (not OBV's personal account).

Public Drive folder — "Anyone with the link · Viewer" OBV Beats / Previews/ lagos-rain-preview.mp3 ← 128 kbps, watermarked sunset-drive-preview.mp3 ... Private Drive folder — "Restricted" OBV Beats / Deliverables/ lagos-rain/ master.wav ← 24-bit / 48 kHz stems.zip ← kick, snare, bass, melody, perc, 808 … license.pdf ← stamped at sale sunset-drive/ ...

Why two folders

  • Sharing model isolation. Public folder is "Anyone with the link" — set once. Private folder is "Restricted" — server grants per-file, per-email shares on each sale.
  • Blast-radius. If the public folder leaks, only watermarked previews are exposed.
  • Revocation. Drive supports time-bound permissions with expirationTime — perfect for the 7-day download window.

Server integration

Server uses the Google Drive API v3 with an OAuth refresh token from the dedicated account. On auction settle:

await drive.permissions.create({ fileId: masterFileId, requestBody: { role: 'reader', type: 'user', emailAddress: buyer.email, expirationTime: new Date(Date.now() + 7*86400*1000).toISOString(), }, }); // Repeat for stems and license PDF. // Then send delivery email with the three Drive view URLs.
Known limit

Google Drive is not a CDN. Files that get popular can hit "download quota exceeded" and lock for 24 hours. Acceptable at MVP traffic; budget to migrate previews to Cloudflare R2 or Bunny.net once we cross ~5,000 unique visitors/month. Masters/stems/licenses can stay on Drive forever — they download once per sale, no quota risk.

Open questions

  1. Should we require a small refundable crypto deposit (e.g. 5% of bid) before placing a bid, to suppress speculative bidding by people who can't actually pay?
  2. Should winners get a "My Wins" page so they can re-download even after the email link expires (within the 7-day window)?
  3. Should we add a "Watch this auction" subscription that emails users 5 min before close?
  4. If a runner-up directly asks "did the original winner pay?", what do we tell them? (current proposed answer: nothing.)
  5. Tax / VAT for international buyers — out-of-scope for v1?
